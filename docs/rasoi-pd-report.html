Section 1

Vision — why this beats HealthifyMe & MyFitnessPal

Existing trackers stop at "log food, see calories." Rasoi treats money as a first-class nutrient and the calendar as a planner, not a diary. Four pillars:

📅

Plan forward, not just log backward

Schedule next week's meals; get the grocery list, cost, and macro totals before the week starts. Logging becomes one tap: "ate as planned."

₹

Money is a first-class nutrient

Every meal shows ₹ beside kcal. "This week: 12,400 kcal, ₹1,850, 480 g protein → ₹3.85 per 10 g protein."

🧺

The pantry is live

140 ml of olive oil left, dying next Thursday at your usage rate → it's already on the shopping list.

🥘

Meals are reusable blocks

"My usual breakfast" is one tap — ingredients, macros and costs flow in automatically.

Section 3

System architecture

Decision: Nuxt 3 full-stack + Supabase. You keep Vue fluency; Supabase gives Firebase-like start speed on real Postgres — so the future "move to Node" is just pointing more servers at the same database, not a rewrite. All business logic lives behind versioned REST (/api/v1) so a mobile client reuses everything.

Section 4

Data model

Two hard conventions protect every rupee and gram: money is integer paise (₹40 = 4000, floats banned) and every ingredient has exactly one canonical unit — g, ml, or piece — with all conversions resolved at write time.

Section 5

The budget engine — FIFO lot costing

Each purchase is a lot. Consumption drains the oldest lot first, so meals are costed at the price of the bag you're actually eating from — which matters in India, where onions can double in a month.

Depletion forecasting

A nightly job computes each ingredient's trailing-28-day usage rate from the ledger: days_left = stock ÷ daily_rate. The pantry screen then reads one cached row per ingredient: "Olive oil — ≈65 ml left, runs out ~1 Aug." Anything dying within a week feeds the shopping list and a WhatsApp low-stock nudge.

Section 6

Calendar & the planned → logged state machine

The single most important invariant in the product. Planned meals show projected macros and cost, but only the flip to logged touches inventory and spend — this is what stops double-counting and phantom pantry depletion.

Typography & components

  • Display: Plus Jakarta Sans 700–800 — friendly and slightly rounded, approachable rather than clinical.
  • Body/UI: Inter 400/500/600 — legible in dense nutrition tables; scale 12/14/16/20/25/31/39 px (1.25 ratio).
  • Numbers: always tabular-nums so kcal and ₹ columns align.
  • Future Hindi: Noto Sans Devanagari fallback; food search accepts transliteration ("palak" ↔ "पालक").
  • Components: 8 px grid, 12–16 px radii cards, progress rings for daily targets, chips for calendar meals, bottom tab bar on mobile (Today · Calendar · Meals · Pantry · Fitness). All pairings pass WCAG AA.

Section 9

Roadmap — six shippable phases (~4 months)

Wk 1–4

1 · Core tracking MVP ships: food diary

Nuxt + Supabase + Drizzle scaffold, auth, ingredient library (~120 Indian seeds), meals library, 5-slot day logging, daily kcal/macro rings.

Wk 5–7

2 · Calendar & planning ships: meal planner

Month/week/day views, planned→logged state machine, drag & copy, slot color themes. Already differentiated from MFP.

Wk 8–11

3 · Budget + pantry — the moat ships: headline product

Purchases, unit calibration, FIFO costing on confirm, ₹ rollups vs budget goals, pantry with run-out forecasts. Start Meta/WhatsApp verification now — it's the long pole.

Wk 12–14

4 · Recipes & fitness

Recipes with steps/photos, recipe→meal conversion, fitness records + custom metrics, BMI/TDEE, trend charts.

Wk 15–17

5 · WhatsApp coach

Phone OTP, opt-in, approved utility templates: daily summary, meal reminders, low-stock and budget alerts.

Wk 18–20

6 · Public hardening

Onboarding wizard, rate limiting, PWA (interim mobile), published OpenAPI spec (the mobile contract), monitoring, privacy & data export.
